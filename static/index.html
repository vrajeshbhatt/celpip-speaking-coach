    Test Guidance & Strategies

    Format & Structure

    The CELPIP Speaking test consists of 8 tasks and takes about 20 minutes to complete. You speak into a microphone and your responses are recorded and evaluated by human raters.

    • Task 1: Giving Advice (90 seconds to speak)
    • Task 2: Talking about a Personal Experience (60 seconds)
    • Task 3: Describing a Scene (60 seconds)
    • Task 4: Making Predictions (60 seconds)
    • Task 5: Comparing and Persuading (60 seconds)
    • Task 6: Dealing with a Difficult Situation (60 seconds)
    • Task 7: Expressing Opinions (90 seconds)
    • Task 8: Describing an Unusual Situation (60 seconds)

    How You Are Evaluated

    CELPIP Raters score your speaking across 4 distinct dimensions:

    Content/Coherence: Number of ideas, quality of descriptions, and logical flow of information.
    Vocabulary: Range of words, precision (using the exact right word), and natural phrasing.
    Listenability: Intonation, rhythm, pronunciation, pauses, and absence of filler words ("um," "uh").
    Task Fulfillment: Did you answer all parts of the prompt? Was your tone appropriate for the situation?

    Task-by-Task Strategies

    Task 1: Giving Advice

    Focus on being helpful and warm. Use modal verbs like "should," "ought to," or "might want to." Structure: Acknowledge problem -> 2-3 suggestions with reasons -> Encouraging closing.

    Task 2: Personal Experience

    Use past tenses accurately. Describe the "Who, What, When, Where" quickly, then focus on the "Why" and your feelings. Aim for a clear narrative arc.

    Task 3: Describing a Scene

    Use spatial prepositions (foreground, background, adjacent to). Describe actions using present continuous ("is walking," "are sitting"). Mention colors, weather, and atmosphere.

    Task 4: Making Predictions

    Use "will," "is going to," or "is likely to." Connect your predictions to visual evidence from Task 3. Example: "Since the boy is reaching for the ball, he will probably throw it next."

    Task 5: Comparing & Persuading

    Acknowledge the other option but emphasize the benefits of your choice. Use comparative adjectives (cheaper, more convenient). Address the "target" person directly by name if given.

    Task 6: Difficult Situation

    Tone is key. Be polite but firm if complaining, or apologetic if canceling plans. Offer a clear alternative or solution. "I understand the situation, however..."

    Task 7: Expressing Opinions

    State your position clearly in the first sentence. Provide 2-3 logical reasons with examples. Acknowledge the counter-argument: "While some might argue X, I believe Y because..."

    Task 8: Unusual Situation

    Describe the strange object or event using metaphors if you don't know the word. "It looks like a..." or "It has the shape of a..." Focus on being descriptive to someone who can't see it.

    How to Achieve CLB 10+

    • Use complex sentence structures (conditionals, relative clauses) rather than just simple sentences.
    • Eliminate filler words completely. Speak at a steady, uninterrupted pace.
    • Use highly specific vocabulary (e.g., "devastated" instead of "very sad", "bustling" instead of "busy").
    • Ensure smooth transitions and explicit signposting ("First of all", "However", "Consequently").
    • Speak naturally with emotion and dynamic intonationâ€”avoid sounding like a robot.
